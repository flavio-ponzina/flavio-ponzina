Efficient Computing Systems Laboratory (ECS Lab)

The Efficient Computing Systems Laboratory (ECS Lab) is a research group focused on building energy- and resource-efficient computing systems, spanning algorithms, software, and architecture. Our goal is to co-design hardware and software to enable practical AI and data-driven applications under tight constraints (compute, memory, energy, and latency), from the edge to larger-scale platforms. At the ECS Lab, I am honored to work with extremely talented and motivated students who are integral to the success of the lab research. I deeply value their growth, and it is a core part of our lab culture to support and celebrate their progress, learning, and development as young researchers.


Research directions we work on at ECS

  • HW–SW co-design and optimization for Edge AI
  • Autonomous, distributed, and efficient learning for space applications
  • Embedded ML optimization for ultra-low power systems
  • Distributed learning in constrained and decentralized settings
  • Processing-in-memory and acceleration on emerging memory technologies
  • Efficient vector search and large-scale data systems
  • Ensemble learning for accurate and robust AI
  • Lifelong learning in connected IoT systems
  • Energy-efficient optimizations of large-scale vector databases
  • Processing-in-memory acceleration
