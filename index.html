About me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at San Diego State University, and director of the Efficient Computing Systems (ECS) Laboratory. After studying Computer Enginnering at Politecnico di Torino in Italy, I obtained my PhD degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne, Switzerland, in 2023. I then joined the SEELab at UC San Diego as a postdoctoral scholar and joined San Diego State University as a faculty memeber in 2025. My research interests include HW-SW co-design optimizations for edge AI, unsupervised, ensemble, and distributed learning, energy-efficient systems, and processing in-memory acceleration on emerging memory technologies.

Research Interests

I am interested in energy-efficient design and HW-SW co-optimization for both ultra-low-power IoT systems and large-scale applications. My current key research directions include:

  • HW-SW co-design for Efficient AI systems

  • Distributed, Unsupervised, and Continual Learning

  • Processing-in-memory acceleration

  • Autonomous AI-based space operations
